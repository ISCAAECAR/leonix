About the Project

Student-built electric race car

LEONIX is more than a car – it’s a full engineering journey. Students design the chassis, calculate gear ratios, wire the electrical system, and capture performance data during practice and race day. The project connects classroom physics, electronics, and programming with real competition pressure on track.

The goal is simple: build a safe, efficient, and competitive EVGP car that can complete the endurance race with the best possible lap consistency, battery strategy, and driver performance.

Technical Specs

Built for endurance & control

External kill switch, main fuse protection, driver harness, and braking system aligned with competition rules.

Data & Strategy
Telemetry Driven

Race decisions are supported by live data: speed, current, voltage, motor temperature, and energy used per lap.

Telemetry & AI

From track data to strategy

LEONIX uses a Cycle Analyst paired with a LoRa transmitter to send real-time performance data from the car to the pit. Voltage, current, speed, and amp-hours are monitored lap by lap, allowing the team to adjust driving style and pit strategy dynamically.

In the next phase, this data can feed an AI model that recommends target lap times, maximum current draw, and optimal pace to finish the race with the best possible position while respecting battery limits.
